2024 Honda Prologue

2024 Honda Prologue

Price Range (starting): $45 000



The 2024 Honda Prologue is Honda's first all-electric SUV, marking the company’s significant step toward a zero-emission future. Designed as a midsize electric SUV, it combines practicality, modern technology, and eco-friendliness, offering a spacious and quiet interior with unique Honda styling while sharing a platform with the Chevrolet Blazer EV.


Exterior Design

  • Body Style: Midsize SUV

  • Dimensions:

    • Length: ~192 inches (approximation based on platform shared with Blazer EV)

    • Wheelbase: 121.8 inches

    • Height: 64.7 inches

    • Width: 74.8 inches


  • Styling Features:

    • Sleek and aerodynamic design with a modern Honda grille.

    • LED headlights and taillights for a premium and futuristic look.

    • Unique wheel designs tailored for improved efficiency.

    • Available in several paint options, including exclusive Prologue colors.



Interior Features

  • Seating: Accommodates 5 passengers comfortably with ample rear legroom.

  • Technology:

    • 11.3-inch central touchscreen infotainment system.

    • 11-inch digital driver display.

    • Wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto as standard.

    • Available premium sound system.


  • Comfort and Quality:

    • High-quality materials, including soft-touch surfaces.

    • Comfortable seating with optional leather upholstery.

    • Large under-floor cargo storage.


  • Practicality:

    • Ample cargo space (though only average for the class).

    • No front trunk (“frunk”) due to platform design.



Performance and Range

  • Powertrain: Electric drivetrain with a single or dual-motor setup (depending on the trim).

  • Estimated Range: 273–296 miles (depending on configuration and driving conditions).

  • Acceleration: Expected 0-60 mph time is competitive but not class-leading.

  • Drive Modes: Includes multiple modes for eco, comfort, and sport driving..



Charging

  • Charging Capabilities:

    • DC fast charging compatibility.

    • Capable of gaining ~70 miles of range in just 10 minutes with a fast charger.

    • Level 2 home charging supported.


  • Battery Technology: Ultium battery system (shared with GM platform).


Safety and Driver Assistance

  • Honda Sensing Suite:

    • Adaptive cruise control.

    • Lane-keeping assist.

    • Automatic emergency braking.

    • Blind-spot monitoring.


  • Advanced Safety:

    • Surround-view camera system (available in higher trims).

    • Rear cross-traffic alert.



Driving Experience

  • Ride Quality: Quiet and smooth, with minimal road and wind noise.

  • Handling: Decent handling but lacks the sportier dynamics Honda is known for.

  • Limitations: Acceleration is adequate but less thrilling compared to some EV rivals.


Trim Levels and Pricing

  • Expected Trims:

    • Base

    • Elite

    • Touring (premium trim)


  • Starting Price: ~$45,000 (before tax incentives).

  • Incentives: Eligible for a $7,500 federal EV tax credit for qualifying buyers.


Competition

The Honda Prologue competes with other midsize electric SUVs, including:

  • Tesla Model Y (better range and tech but more expensive).

  • Ford Mustang Mach-E (sportier dynamics but less cargo space).

  • Hyundai Ioniq 5 (more futuristic design and faster charging).

  • Chevrolet Blazer EV (shares the same platform but with a different design).

Additional packages and features can increase the price significantly.


Pros and Cons

  • Pros:

    • Spacious and quiet interior.

    • User-friendly infotainment system.

    • Eligible for EV incentives.

    • Decent range for daily commutes and family use.


  • Cons:

    • Lacks a high-performance variant.

    • Cargo space is average compared to competitors.

    • Acceleration and driving dynamics are underwhelming.



Release Date

The 2024 Honda Prologue is expected to arrive at dealerships in early 2024. This SUV represents Honda’s commitment to electrification, offering a practical and eco-friendly solution for modern families and individuals transitioning to electric vehicles.
